Vehicle

Test Drive
  • A very important aspect of buying a vehicle is the test drive. Starting the vehicle should be a very smooth process.
  • Gear shifting should be effortless, you should not apply any extra pressure on the stick.
  • Engage the clutch and when you release it, the vehicle should move forward in a gentle manner without jerks or vibrations.
  • Take the car out for a ride, feel the suspension, no squeaking or odd vibrations should be felt.

Document Verification
  • Registration Certificate is a crucial document that confirms the legality of the vehicle and ownership.
  • VIN, Chassis no. and Engine no. should be the same everywhere i.e., on the Registration Certificate and the vehicle.
  • Insurance is another important vehicle document without which a vehicle cannot ply on the roads.
  • A Loan NOC is something that you should be aware of in case the vehicle was purchased on a loan.
  • Pollution under control certificate shows the emission levels of the vehicle, via which you can ascertain the condition the engine is in.

Engine Checking
  • It is suggested to inspect the engine thoroughly.
  • Check for leaks, corrosion, and cracked tubes.
  • Also, look at the oil and transmission fluid using a dipstick.

Tyres Checking
  • The condition of the tyres can be good or bad depending on the usage.
  • You can check for the uniformity of the tyres concerning wear and tear.
  • If the tyres are not worn evenly, it can lead to alignment issues, which means the car might tend to drift in a direction while driving.

Other Checking
  • If the car is not too old but has major mileage issues, then it can be a result of deeper problems.
  • Car owners make modifications to their vehicle. If the modifications are illegal, it can create complications for you.
  • Some vehicles may also have outdated safety features and lack the fuel efficiency found in newer models

Mobile

Condition of the phone
  • Take a close look at the phone for any signs of damage, such as scratches, dents, or cracks.
  • Ask the seller about the phone’s history. Has it been in any accidents?
  • If the phone is in poor condition, it may not be worth the price.

Device Information
  • Check the IMEI number. The IMEI number is a unique identifier for your phone.
  • Make sure the phone is running the latest version of Android/IOS.
  • Check the battery health of the phone.

Display and Camera
  • Check the photo and video quality of camera.
  • Check the display from all the corners.
  • Check the smoothness of display.

Bill and Box
  • Check the original bill of phonen before buying.
  • Also ask for box of phone with it's accessories.
  • Also ask about the warranty of phone.

Electronics

Inspect the Physical Condition
  • When inspecting used electronics, be sure to check for any scratches, dents, or cracks on the surface.
  • These physical damages can affect the functionality of the electronic and may also indicate that it wasn't well taken care of by its previous owner.
  • Look out for missing buttons, ports or any other essential components that could affect its performance.
  • If possible, test the electronic to ensure that all of its functions are working properly.

History and Proof of Purchase
  • Don't be afraid to ask the seller questions about the history of the used electronic you're interested in.
  • This could include how long they've had it, how often it was used, and if it has ever been repaired.
  • Ask for proof of purchase to ensure that the electronic was not stolen or illegally obtained.

Age of Electronic
  • The age of a used electronic is an important factor to consider when buying one.
  • Older electronics may be cheaper, but they also have a higher chance of malfunctioning or becoming obsolete.
  • It's recommended to buy used electronics that are less than two years old, as they have a longer lifespan and are more likely to be compatible with current technology.

Furniture

Check for Damages
  • It might seem obvious but this is the single bit of advice that all the experts suggest.
  • While you might have fallen in love with a piece of furniture, make sure you still use your head when purchasing it and, if there is damage, think about how fixable it is.
  • If the damage doesn’t look like it can be fixed, you might need to walk away.

Look for Labels
  • Just like you would when buying clothes, make sure you look out for labels on second hand furniture.
  • Doing a quick online search for a tag or label you’re not familiar with can provide a lot of information about the quality of the manufacturer and the value of the piece.

Check the Condition
  • Most second hand furniture will contain some kind of wood and it’s crucial that you check it’s in good condition before buying.
  • Research and look at the type of wood when buying a second hand wooden piece, as this can depend on how durable the item will be.
  • If you're looking for the lasting factor then opt for hardwoods such as oak or maple as these make strong and long-lasting pieces of furniture.
